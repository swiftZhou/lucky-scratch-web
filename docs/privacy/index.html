Privacy Policy

App: Lucky Scratch  |  Effective date: June 25, 2026  |  Last updated: June 25, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Lucky Scratch ("we," "us," or "our") handles information when you use our mobile application Lucky Scratch (the "App"). By downloading or using the App, you agree to this Privacy Policy.

Entertainment only — not real-money gambling. Lucky Scratch is a casual entertainment app with virtual scratch-off tickets and in-app coins. Coins and prizes have no cash value, cannot be redeemed for real money or real-world goods, and do not constitute gambling under applicable law. The App does not offer real-money wagering, payouts, or prizes of monetary value.

1. Summary

  • We do not require an account, email address, phone number, or other personal identifiers to use the App.
  • In the current version (1.0), gameplay progress and settings are stored locally on your device only.
  • We do not sell your personal information.
  • We do not use the App to collect precise location, contacts, photos, microphone recordings, or health data.
  • Optional rewarded advertising may be introduced in a future update; Section 6 explains how that would work.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information stored on your device (local data)

To provide gameplay and remember your preferences, the App stores the following data locally on your device using iOS storage (UserDefaults). This data stays on your device unless you delete the App or reset your device:

  • Virtual coin balance and gameplay statistics (for example, tickets played, tickets won, total virtual winnings)
  • Sound effects and haptic feedback settings
  • Whether optional ads have been removed (if that feature is offered in a future version)
  • Date of your last played ticket and daily free-ticket claim status

This information is used solely to operate the App and is not transmitted to our servers in version 1.0, because we do not operate user accounts or cloud save in this version.

2.2 Information we do not collect directly

In version 1.0, we do not directly collect:

  • Name, email address, postal address, or phone number
  • Payment card or banking information (the App does not process real-money payments in version 1.0)
  • Precise geolocation
  • Contacts, calendars, photos, or files
  • Microphone or camera recordings

2.3 Device permissions and on-device features

The App uses standard iOS audio and haptic capabilities to play sound effects and vibration while you scratch tickets. These features run on your device. We do not record your voice or ambient audio. You can disable sound and haptic feedback in Settings inside the App.

2.4 Information collected automatically by Apple

Apple may collect certain diagnostic, analytics, or crash data through iOS and the App Store according to your device settings and Apple's policies. That collection is controlled by Apple, not by us. Please review Apple's privacy documentation for details.

3. How We Use Information

We use locally stored App data only to:

  • Provide and maintain gameplay (virtual coins, tickets, and statistics)
  • Remember your preferences (sound and haptics)
  • Enforce daily free-ticket limits and similar in-app rules
  • Improve stability and user experience within the App

We do not use locally stored data for cross-app tracking or to build advertising profiles in version 1.0.

4. Legal Bases (where applicable)

Where privacy laws require a legal basis, we process information as necessary to perform our contract with you (providing the App), based on your choices (such as enabling sound or haptics), and where applicable, based on our legitimate interests in operating and improving a safe entertainment product.

5. How We Share Information

We do not sell or share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising. In version 1.0, we do not transmit your local gameplay data to third parties.

We may disclose information only if required to:

  • Comply with applicable law, regulation, legal process, or enforceable governmental request
  • Protect the rights, safety, and security of users, the public, or our App
  • Investigate fraud, security issues, or misuse of the App

6. Third-Party Services

6.1 Privacy Policy link (in-App browser)

When you tap "Privacy Policy" in Settings, the App opens this page in an in-app browser (Safari View Controller). Your visit to this website may be subject to standard web server logs maintained by our hosting provider (for example, IP address, browser type, and access time). We use such logs only for security and basic site operation.

6.2 Advertising (future update — not active in version 1.0)

A future version of the App may offer optional rewarded video advertisements through Google AdMob. Version 1.0 does not display third-party ads and does not integrate the AdMob SDK. If and when ads are enabled, we will update this Privacy Policy and, where required, request your permission before using data for tracking (for example, through Apple's App Tracking Transparency prompt on iOS).

If AdMob is enabled in a future version, Google may collect or receive information such as:

  • Device identifiers (for example, advertising identifier where permitted)
  • IP address and general device information
  • Ad interaction data (for example, impressions and completions for rewarded ads)

Google's use of data is governed by Google's policies. You can learn more at https://policies.google.com/privacy and manage ad personalization in your device settings.

7. Data Retention

Local gameplay data remains on your device until you delete the App or clear App data by reinstalling. We do not retain copies of your local gameplay data on our servers in version 1.0. Web server logs, if any, are retained only as long as reasonably necessary for security and operations, then deleted or anonymized.

8. Your Privacy Rights (United States)

Depending on where you live, you may have rights under state privacy laws, including the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), as amended by the CPRA, and similar laws in states such as Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, and Utah.

Because version 1.0 does not collect personal information on our servers, many requests may be limited to:

  • Right to know: This Policy describes our practices.
  • Right to delete: Uninstalling the App deletes locally stored gameplay data from your device.
  • Right to opt out of sale/sharing: We do not sell or share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising.
  • Right to non-discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising privacy rights.

California residents may also have the right to limit use of sensitive personal information where applicable. We do not collect sensitive personal information through the App in version 1.0.

To submit a privacy request, contact us using the information in Section 13. We may need to verify your request as allowed by law. You may designate an authorized agent where applicable.

9. Children's Privacy (COPPA)

Lucky Scratch is intended for a general audience and is not directed to children under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has provided personal information to us, please contact us and we will take appropriate steps to delete it.

Parents and guardians should supervise children's use of mobile apps. Because the App uses virtual coins and simulated lottery-style gameplay, we recommend parental guidance for younger users.

10. Security

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational measures designed to protect information handled through the App. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure. Because gameplay data in version 1.0 is stored locally, you should also protect your device with a passcode, Face ID, or Touch ID.

11. International Users

The App is offered in the United States and may be available in other regions through the App Store. If you use the App outside the United States, your information may be processed according to this Policy and applicable local law. Where required, we will provide additional notices or choices.

12. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time, for example when we add advertising, in-app purchases, cloud save, analytics, or other features. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date above and provide additional notice in the App or on this page where required by law. Your continued use of the App after changes become effective means you accept the updated Policy.
